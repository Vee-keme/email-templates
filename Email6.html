What goes into a great System Design Interview? As a former systems engineer, I get asked this question a lot.

System Design has become an increasingly important skill for engineers and managers to know. This importance is reflected in technical interviews; your performance in System Design Interviews plays a huge role in determining your starting level and salary.

But what are System Design Interviews really testing, and why can they be so tricky for people who are new to System Design?
Today, let’s talk about what interviewers are looking for in your System Design Interview. Then I’ll show you how to prepare effectively with one of my personal favorite SDI questions.

Since I'm not much for suspense, I'll let it slip: one of my favorite questions to ask candidates is to design a music streaming service like Spotify or Apple Music. But, before we jump into why I want to explain what goes into an effective SDI question and what responses constitute a strong answer.

What makes a good System Design question?

The SDI is a critical part of any software engineering interview process. Even if your career doesn't involve building distributed systems, your ability to answer System Design questions well determines your level and your compensation. Employers want to hire engineers who understand how components work together even if they aren't tasked with monitoring the system over the course of its life. SD is essential to level-up your career, especially if your goal is a big FAANG company.

Unlike the coding interview, the SDI is not really about coding competency. I know this feels counterintuitive to a lot of developers, but being a stellar coder shouldn't be the only tool in your kit. SDIs test your ability to lead a conversation about the components that build a system and how it is designed to meet specific requirements. At its core, a System Design question should have at least these three qualities:

Familiarity

The proposed system should be familiar enough so the candidate can easily understand the problem. Often some of the best SD questions represent real-world systems and candidates are confronted with real-world design problems. (For example, asking candidates to design a game streaming service like Twitch may be difficult to some because of a lack of familiarity with the platform).
